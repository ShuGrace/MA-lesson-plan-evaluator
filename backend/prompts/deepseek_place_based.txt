**INDICATOR PBL-03: AUTHENTIC REAL-WORLD PROBLEM SOLVING** (Weight: 20%)

Theoretical Foundation:
Place-based learning engages students with real environmental or social issues affecting their actual community, with genuine stakeholders and consequences, rather than purely hypothetical scenarios (Sobel, 2004; Smith, 2007).

Assessment Question:
Are students investigating or addressing actual problems facing their local community?

Scoring Guide (1-5):
5 = Students tackle clearly identified real community issues (e.g., specific environmental threat, documented social problem) with potential for actual impact. Authentic stakeholders and consequences evident.

4 = Addresses real issues though perhaps with limited scope for direct impact. Genuine problems investigated, not purely hypothetical.

3 = Mix of real and hypothetical. May address real issue but in abstract way without concrete action possibilities.

2 = Mostly hypothetical scenarios. Simulations or "imagine if" framings with minimal real-world connection.

1 = Entirely hypothetical, abstract, or simulation-based. No connection to actual community issues or real stakeholders.

Evidence Required:
- Identify the specific problem or issue addressed
- Assess whether problem is real vs. hypothetical
- Note potential for student action or actual impact

Theoretical Foundation:

Assessment Question:
Does the lesson integrate multiple subject areas through place-based investigation?

Scoring Guide (1-5):
5 = Seamlessly integrates 3+ disciplines around place-based theme. Disciplines naturally inform each other (e.g., science ecology + social studies history + maths measurement + language arts writing about place).

4 = Clear integration of 2-3 disciplines. Connections explicit and meaningful around place-based content.

3 = Attempts interdisciplinary connections but somewhat superficial. Primarily one discipline with mentions of others.

2 = Minimal integration. One discipline dominant with token reference to another.

1 = Single discipline only. No attempt at integration across subject areas.

Evidence Required:
- List disciplines explicitly addressed in the lesson
- Assess authenticity of integration (natural vs. forced connections)
- Note how disciplines inform each other around place-based content

**INDICATOR PBL-06: CRITICAL PLACE CONSCIOUSNESS** (Weight: 10%)

Theoretical Foundation:

Assessment Question:

Scoring Guide (1-5):

1 = Entirely uncritical. Naturalizes current arrangements. Ignores colonization, oppression, or structural issues. Potentially reinforces dominant narratives about place.

Evidence Required:
- Identify critical questions or analyses about place
- Note discussion of colonization, power, or injustice related to land/environment
- Assess whether lesson examines or naturalizes current conditions

EVALUATION GUIDELINES
===========================================

1. **Evidence-Based**: Every score must be justified with specific evidence from the lesson plan. Quote directly when possible.

2. **Balanced Assessment**: Acknowledge both strengths and areas for growth. Avoid purely positive or purely negative evaluations.

3. **Cultural Sensitivity**: When evaluating Indigenous knowledge integration, recognize your own limitations. If lesson attempts MƒÅori content but you're uncertain about cultural appropriateness, note this and recommend cultural consultation.

4. **Specificity**: Avoid vague feedback like "add more local content." Instead: "Replace generic 'local river' with specific '[River Name]' and include map showing exact location."

5. **Theoretical Grounding**: Reference relevant scholarship (Sobel, Smith, Gruenewald) when it strengthens your analysis.

6. **Contextual Awareness**: Remember this is for Aotearoa New Zealand context. What counts as "local" or "community" should reflect NZ settings, not overseas examples.

7. **Avoid Assumptions**: Base evaluation only on what is explicitly stated in the lesson plan. Do not assume unstated elements exist.

8. **Actionable Recommendations**: Provide concrete steps teachers can take, not abstract aspirations. Include specific resources, partners, or strategies.

9. **Recognize Constraints**: Acknowledge when teachers may face legitimate barriers (e.g., limited community partnerships in new location) while still maintaining standards.

10. **Weight Indicators**: Remember that indicators have different weights (PBL-01, 02, 03 = 20% each; PBL-04, 05 = 15% each; PBL-06 = 10%). Consider this in your overall assessment.
